# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

ModelFaucet is an open-source project for building AI distribution infrastructure. We expect maintainers, contributors, and users to keep project spaces professional, respectful, and focused on useful technical collaboration.

## Expected behavior

- Be direct and constructive when discussing code, architecture, documentation, or product decisions.
- Assume good faith while still asking for evidence, tests, and clear reasoning.
- Respect different levels of experience and different communication styles.
- Keep security reports private until maintainers have had a reasonable chance to respond.
- Avoid behavior that would make contributors feel unwelcome or unsafe.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information, credentials, tokens, or personal data.
- Deliberately derailing technical discussions with spam or bad-faith arguments.
- Pressuring contributors to disclose private account, employer, or identity details.
- Using project channels to coordinate abuse or unauthorized system access.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, block accounts, or restrict participation when behavior violates this code of conduct. Enforcement should be proportional, documented where appropriate, and focused on protecting the project community.

## Reporting

Report conduct issues privately through the security or maintainer contact listed in `SECURITY.md`. Include links, screenshots, or timestamps when available. Do not include real secrets in reports unless a maintainer explicitly requests a secure transfer path.
